Snowdops

"Do you know what I was, how I lived? You know what despair is; then winter should have meaning for you. I did not expect to survive, earth suppressing me. I didn't expect to waken again, to feel in damp earth my body able to respond again, remembering after so long how to open again in the cold light of the earliest spring--- afraid, yes, but among you again crying yes risk joy in the raw wind of the new world." - Gluck, Page 6

Spring Snow

"Look at the night sky: I have two selves, two kinds of power. I am here with you, at the window, watching you react. Yesterday the moon rose over moist earth in the lower garden. Now the earth glitters like the moon, like dead matter crusted with light. You can close your eyes now. I have heard your cries, and cries before yours, and the demand behind them. I have shown you what you want: not belief, but capitulation to authority, which depends on violence." - Gluck, Page 9

Scilla

"Not I, you idiot, not self, but we, we-- waves of sky blue like a critique of heaven: why do you treasure your voice when to be one thing is to be the next to nothing? Why do you look up? To hear an echo like the voice of god? You are all the same to us, solitary, standing above us, planning your silly lives: you go where you are sent, like all things, where the wind plants you, one or another of you forever looking down and seeing some image of water, and hearing what? Waves, and over waves, birds singing." - Gluck, Page 14

Snowdrop

This poem is very motivating and moving to me. To think that when everyting in the world is trying to bring you down and that the odds are against you, that in the end everything will be okay and you will always wake up to a new day, in a new world.

Spring Snow

This poem was very hard to decypher and understand for me. From what I can understand, being free in the world requires a lot of effort and tears in order to achieve. This is something that many of us take for granted while other people in other parts of the world have to resort to voilence against authority in order to achieve this level of relative freedom.

Scilla

Although to me this poem had no direct message, the flower had a very strong tone with a very negative view towards humans and how we live our lives. Although the flower is literally planted underneath our feet, metaphorically the flower is looking down on us.
